THEATRE/DRAMA

WILLIAM SHAKESPEARE’S HAMLET

Theatrics and drama have ways been endearing throughout my academic life. Being in high school, I was an active member of the literary club and often got my articles, poems and short stories published in newspapers as well as School magazine covers. I am an enthusiastic debater apart from my passion for writing. During the literary carnival, a week-long event where drama/ theatrical contest was conducted in school for secondary students, I enacted the king’s role and also voiced the narration in William Shakespeare’s renowned tragic play, Hamlet. I greatly rejoiced the deep expression of emotion including immense pleasure and the guilt of murder and death. It had been a delightful task recreating the script with my own vision and wordplay. I ensured that I placed the best extracts from “Hamlet” emphasising the core emotion of deceit and tragedy. On the basis of research and analysis, I discovered the overwhelming ideals of Shakespearean comedies which are :-
mistaken identities often involving disguise
A witty servant
Separation and reunification
Relationships and conflicts
As a group of eleven, my play mates and I sorted out the best-suited roles for each of us through auditions. Another team of four set out to craft the props including swords and crowns while some did the makeup and costumes. Among 200 participants, our team secured the first place for the script and costumes leaving an indelible memory in our minds. The script had been published in the School magazine under the literature columns as well.
